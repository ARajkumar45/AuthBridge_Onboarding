LEAVE POLICY — ACME CORPORATION (Tenant: AuthBridge)
Effective Date: January 1, 2026 | Version 3.2 | Approved by: CHRO Office

1. SCOPE
This policy applies to all full-time employees of Acme Corporation onboarded through the AuthBridge AI-Native Onboarding Platform. Contract workers and interns follow separate guidelines.

2. LEAVE ENTITLEMENTS (per calendar year)
- Earned Leave (EL): 24 days per year, accruing at 2 days per month. Encashment of up to 10 unused days permitted at year-end.
- Casual Leave (CL): 12 days per year. Cannot be carried forward. Maximum 3 consecutive days.
- Sick Leave (SL): 12 days per year. Medical certificate required for absences exceeding 2 consecutive days.
- Maternity Leave: 26 weeks for the first two children (as per Maternity Benefit Act, 2017). 12 weeks for third child onwards.
- Paternity Leave: 15 working days within 6 months of child's birth or adoption.
- Bereavement Leave: 5 days for immediate family (spouse, children, parents, siblings).

3. PROBATION PERIOD LEAVE
New hires during the 6-month probation period receive:
- CL: 1 day per month (6 total during probation)
- SL: 1 day per month (6 total during probation)
- EL does not accrue during probation; it begins from the 7th month.
- All other statutory leaves (maternity, paternity) apply from Day 1.

4. LEAVE APPLICATION PROCESS
- All leave requests must be submitted through the HRMS portal at least 3 working days in advance for planned leave.
- Sick leave of 3+ days requires uploading a medical certificate to the onboarding document portal.
- Manager approval is required for all leave types. Auto-approval applies for CL of 1 day if submitted 48 hours in advance.
- The AI onboarding assistant can help new hires understand their leave balance, policy details, and application process.

5. PUBLIC HOLIDAYS
- 10 national/regional holidays per year as declared by the company.
- 2 optional/floater holidays (employee's choice from a pre-approved list).
- Holiday calendar is shared during onboarding and available in the policy portal.

6. LEAVE WITHOUT PAY (LWP)
- Permitted only after exhaustion of all paid leave balances.
- Requires written approval from Department Head and HR.
- LWP exceeding 30 days in a year may affect annual appraisal and benefits eligibility.

7. COMPLIANCE NOTE
This policy complies with the Factories Act 1948, Shops and Establishments Act (state-specific), and the Code on Social Security 2020. All leave records are maintained as per statutory requirements with a minimum retention period of 8 years.
