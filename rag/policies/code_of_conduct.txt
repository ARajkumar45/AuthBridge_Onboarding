CODE OF CONDUCT AND ETHICS POLICY — ACME CORPORATION (Tenant: AuthBridge)
Effective Date: January 1, 2026 | Version 2.3 | Approved by: Board of Directors

1. PURPOSE
This Code of Conduct establishes the ethical standards and behavioral expectations for all employees of Acme Corporation. Acknowledgement of this code is a mandatory step in the onboarding process, tracked by the AI-Native Onboarding system.

2. CORE VALUES
- Integrity: We act honestly and ethically in all business dealings.
- Respect: We treat all colleagues, clients, and stakeholders with dignity.
- Accountability: We take responsibility for our actions and decisions.
- Innovation: We embrace technology and continuous improvement.
- Compliance: We adhere to all applicable laws and regulations.

3. WORKPLACE CONDUCT
3.1 Professional Behavior: Employees must maintain professional conduct during work hours and at company events. This includes respectful communication, punctuality, and appropriate dress code.
3.2 Anti-Harassment: Zero tolerance for harassment of any kind — sexual, verbal, physical, or cyber. All complaints handled per the Prevention of Sexual Harassment (POSH) Act, 2013. Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) contactable via the HRMS portal.
3.3 Diversity and Inclusion: Acme Corporation is committed to a diverse, equitable, and inclusive workplace. Discrimination based on gender, caste, religion, disability, age, sexual orientation, or any protected characteristic is strictly prohibited.

4. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
- Employees must disclose any personal or financial interest that may conflict with company interests.
- Outside employment requires written approval from the Department Head.
- Gifts from vendors/clients exceeding ₹5,000 in value must be declared to the Ethics Committee.

5. CONFIDENTIALITY AND DATA PROTECTION
- All company information, client data, and proprietary technology are confidential.
- NDA signed during onboarding applies for 2 years post-employment.
- Personal data handling must comply with the DPDP Act 2023.
- Sharing company data on personal devices, cloud storage, or social media is prohibited without authorization.

6. USE OF COMPANY RESOURCES
- Company assets (laptops, phones, vehicles) are for business use only.
- Limited personal use of email and internet is permitted but monitored per IT policy.
- Software installations require IT approval to maintain security compliance.

7. REPORTING AND WHISTLEBLOWER PROTECTION
- Employees must report any violation of this code to their manager, HR, or the Ethics Hotline.
- Whistleblower protection: Employees reporting in good faith are protected from retaliation per the Whistleblower Protection Act.
- Anonymous reporting available through the Ethics Hotline: ethics@acmecorp.in

8. COMPLIANCE AND CONSEQUENCES
- Acknowledgement of this policy is mandatory during onboarding (tracked in the tasks table).
- Annual re-certification required for all employees.
- Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
- Serious violations (fraud, theft, harassment) may lead to legal proceedings.

9. ONBOARDING REQUIREMENT
- New hires must read and acknowledge this Code of Conduct within the first 3 days of joining.
- The AI onboarding assistant will guide new hires through key sections and answer questions.
- Acknowledgement is digitally recorded in the onboarding system with timestamp and IP address.
